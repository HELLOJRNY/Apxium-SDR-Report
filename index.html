AU — ABE Follow-Up Script ✓ Working
1
Opening — Conference Reference
Warm intro using ABE conference as shared context. Immediate recognition shortens the trust gap.
"Hi [Name], this is [SDR] from Apxium — we had a presence at the ABE conference earlier this year, and I'm reaching out to some of the firms we connected with there."
2
Problem Hook — AR Burden
Lead with the pain point, not the product. ASIC compliance pressure adds urgency for AU firms.
"A lot of accounting firms we're speaking to are spending 5–10 hours a week manually chasing overdue invoices — and with ASIC tightening compliance expectations, it's only adding to the pressure."
3
Solution Intro — Apxium
Brief, clear. What Apxium does — not how it does it.
"Apxium integrates directly with your practice management software to automate debtor follow-up — so your team gets the time back without losing control."
4
Value Proof Point
Concrete outcome to anchor value. Keeps the conversation from becoming abstract.
"Firms using Apxium typically reduce debtor days by around 30% in the first quarter — without any change to their billing workflow."
5
CTA — 15-Minute Demo
Soft ask. Low friction. Positions the call as exploratory, not a hard sell.
"Would it make sense to grab 15 minutes to see how it works for a firm like yours? No pitch — just a quick look at what it could save you."
6
Objection Handling
Three branch paths depending on response received.
Timing
"Totally understand — can I check back with you after May 15 once the tax crunch is through?"
Not Interested
"Is that more about timing, or is it more about the solution itself? Happy to leave it if it's not a fit."
Has a Tool
"What are you using? A lot of firms we speak to have something in place but are still spending significant time on manual follow-up."
US — Cold Outreach Script ✗ Needs Rebuild
1
Opening — Cold Intro Issue
No shared context. No brand recognition. Prospects have never heard of Apxium before the call.
"Hi [Name], this is [SDR] from Apxium — we help accounting firms automate their accounts receivable…" [prospect has no context for who Apxium is]
2
Problem Hook — AU References Critical Issue
Script still references ASIC and ABE — both meaningless to US accounting firms. This is where trust breaks down immediately.
"…with ASIC tightening compliance…" / "…from the ABE conference…" — US prospects don't know what ASIC or ABE are. Kills credibility instantly.
3
Solution Intro — No US Context Issue
Solution introduction is identical to AU version with no adaptation for US market dynamics, terminology, or pain points.
No US case studies, no reference to US firm pain points (state-level compliance, US billing software ecosystems), no localised proof points.
4
Value Proof — No US Proof Points Issue
AU outcome statistics quoted without any US-specific data or comparable firm references.
US firms need US references. "Firms in Australia reduce debtor days…" lands poorly in California.
5
CTA — Too Direct for Cold US Issue
A direct 15-minute demo ask is too aggressive for a cold US prospect who has zero prior Apxium awareness. Needs a softer, education-first CTA.
Suggested replacement: "Would it be worth sending over a one-pager on how it works? Then we can see if a quick call makes sense."
6
Objection Handling — No US Branches Issue
Objection handling hasn't been adapted for US-specific responses. 6 hang-ups in this campaign suggest the framing is not landing before objections are even surfaced.
Hang Up
6 calls disconnected before any objection was surfaced. Brand awareness issue, not script.
Not Interested
6 calls. No specific reason captured. Need an objection probe step in the US version.
IVR / Gate
2 calls blocked by IVR/gatekeepers. List structure (firm vs office-level) is the fix here.
US Script — Issues to Fix Before Next Cycle
🔴
Remove ASIC references. ASIC is the Australian Securities & Investments Commission — completely irrelevant to US accounting firms. Replace with relevant US regulatory context (IRS compliance, state-level filing requirements).
🔴
Remove ABE references. ABE is an AU-specific conference. US prospects have no context for this and it makes the intro feel off. Replace with a credibility-first opener.
🟡
Add US proof points. Apxium needs at least 1–2 US firm references or US-relevant statistics before this script can convert. If none exist yet, use anonymised data or industry benchmarks.
🟡
Soften the CTA. A direct demo ask on a cold call to a US firm with zero brand awareness is too aggressive. Intermediate step (send a resource, offer a brief intro call) will get more traction.
🟡
Rebuild the list at office level. The Top 500 list is at firm level, meaning SDRs can't navigate the IVR/gatekeeper for multi-office organisations. Office-level records are required before calling resumes.
